01 · Brand Essence

Elevated, without the attitude.

Jackson House is a neighbourhood restaurant that was named Delaware's Best New Restaurant in 2025 — and it hasn't changed the wallpaper because of it. The brand voice is warm and confident, the room is low-lit and gilded, and the menu reads like an 1890s tavern card with contemporary technique underneath. Nothing here should look like it was designed in 2026.

Every design decision must earn one of three adjectives — elevated, prestige, or refined. If the asset can also be described as trendy, playful, or generic, pull it.

Voice

Confident · Warm · Understated

Mood

Gilded · Low-lit · Heritage

Promise

Best-of-class food, no pretension

Positioning statement

For Middletown diners who want a restaurant that respects their Friday night, Jackson House serves seasonal American food and artisanal cocktails in a warmly-lit room — the only Best New Restaurant in Delaware that still feels like the neighbourhood's.

02 · Logo

The cartouche.

The Jackson House mark is an ornate cartouche — a gilded nameplate drawn from 19th-century apothecary and tavern signage. It exists in three executions. Always use the execution that matches the background, never invert the foil colour.

Primary — black line on parchment
Gold foil — on ink or dark imagery
Gold foil lockup — for stationery, menus, signage

Clearspace

Minimum clearspace around the cartouche equals the height of the word "HOUSE" on all four sides. Never crop the scrollwork.

02 · Logo continued

Incorrect usage.

The cartouche is the single most-protected asset in the Jackson House system. Every rule below exists because breaking it reads as counterfeit.

Do not place gold foil on pure white — it reads synthetic. Use ink or parchment.
Do not place the mark on coloured fills outside the palette.
Do not rotate, skew, or distort the cartouche.
Do not add shadows, glows, or bevels.

Minimum size

  • Digital: 180px wide (the scrollwork stops being legible below this)
  • Print: 1.25" / 32mm wide
  • Instagram avatar: use a simplified "JH" monogram variant — the full cartouche is illegible at 110px

03 · Colour

Ink, foil, and parchment.

A three-tone palette with one warm-dark accent. Ivory parchment replaces pure white in every context — gold foil on white reads counterfeit. Black anchors the gold so it reads upscale, not flashy.

Usage ratios

  • 60% Parchment or Ink (whichever is the canvas)
  • 25% the opposing base (ink on parch, parch on ink)
  • 12% Antique Gold — headlines, rules, ornaments
  • 3% Aged Brass — captions and fine print only

Do not introduce a fifth colour. Seasonal campaigns may tint photography, not the palette.

04 · Typography

Four voices, one letterpress.

The type system echoes a letterpress menu card. Rye stamps the name, Playfair Display carries the menu, Cormorant Italic softens the quotes, Inter handles the legal. Never mix in a fifth face.

05 · Voice & Tone

Confident. Never chatty.

Every Jackson House caption follows the same rhythm: the dish name with a period, one punchy line, the address, the CTA. The punchy line may be declarative, a nod, or a one-word answer. It is never an explanation. The image explains the dish.

Dilly Dally Dirty Martini. 🍸You already know.
Burrata. 🍅Some things don't need to be complicated.
Cornbread Muffins. 🧈Warm out of the oven. Butter melts on contact.
Fried Catfish Fingers. 🐟Crispy, golden, mustard dipping sauce. Southern comfort, Jackson House style.

Do

  • Start with the dish name + period + emoji
  • Keep punchline under 10 words when possible
  • Reference the Best New Delaware award sparingly
  • Leave space — one short line is stronger than three

Don't

  • Write the ingredient list unless it's a brand-new dish
  • Use marketing-speak: "amazing", "must-try", "foodie"
  • Quote the chef — the food is the chef
  • Use more than one emoji per caption

06 · Photography

Warm, low-key, one hero.

Photography is the single largest on-brand signal Jackson House controls. All imagery should look like it was shot inside the restaurant at 7:30pm on a Thursday — warm tungsten light, shallow depth, one hero per frame.

Lighting direction

  • Warm tungsten / candlelight (2700–3200K)
  • Single key light, low angle, strong falloff
  • Lift shadows only to ink-black — never grey-black
  • Gold highlights on rim of glassware and plate edges are desirable

Composition

  • One focal dish or drink, centred or rule-of-thirds
  • Overhead for shareable boards; 3/4 angle for mains; low for cocktails
  • Negative space on at least one side for caption/name overlay
  • Never stack multiple dishes in the hero frame — save for carousel slides

Forbidden

  • Flash-lit food. Ever.
  • Cool white backgrounds, marble flatlays, pastel props
  • Hands-in-shot unless the craft is the subject (pouring, plating, garnishing)
  • Generic bokeh overlays or colour filters applied post-shoot

When in doubt: if the photo would look at home in a Francis Mallmann cookbook, it's on-brand. If it belongs on a delivery app, it isn't.

08 · Social Specs & Standards

The template ruleset.

Canvas

  • Feed: 4:5 · 1080 × 1350 px
  • Reels cover: 9:16 · 1080 × 1920 px
  • Story: 9:16 · 1080 × 1920 px

Safe area

  • 48px margin on all sides (feed)
  • 180px top / 280px bottom safe area on Reels (avoids UI)

Caption structure

[Dish/Drink Name]. [emoji]
[One punchy line.]

Posting schedule

  • Monday: images (food or cocktails)
  • Friday: images (food or cocktails)
  • Wednesday: BTS / culture / reel

Asset checklist before every post

  • ☐ Photo is warm-lit, single-focus, negative space on one side
  • ☐ Drink/dish name in Rye ≥48pt, left-aligned, on ink or parchment (never pure white)
  • ☐ Tagline ≤10 words, Cormorant Italic or Playfair
  • ☐ One ornament max — scrollwork flourish only from the logo family
  • ☐ Footer includes address + CTA
  • ☐ No emojis beyond the one in the caption
