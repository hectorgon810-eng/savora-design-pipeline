## 2026-04-23 — Round 1 review (50 images, 0×5★ / 6×4★)

Main shift: **stop single-image generation. Plan CAROUSELS first.**

### THE CAROUSEL-FIRST RULE

Current waste: generating isolated images, then trying to assemble posts afterward. Burns credits re-generating for fit.

**New workflow:**
1. Decide the post concept (e.g., "Chef's Picks").
2. Plan the carousel — number of slides (2–5), what each slide is, how they flow.
3. Generate only what that carousel needs.
4. Ship as a set.

### CAROUSEL FORMATS

**A. Title-card + dish images**
- Slide 1: text card "CHEF'S PICKS" (or similar)
- Slides 2–5: individual dish images (Elote Burger, etc.)

**B. Diagonal-split single frame**
- One image containing TWO dishes — top-left / bottom-right split.
- Composition discipline: hero dish CENTERED in its half. No empty dead space. No cropping subjects out.
- Use when two dishes share a theme (e.g., two starters, two burgers).

**C. Standalone dish**
- Not everything needs a text card. A strong "Elote Burger" image alone is valid.
- Use for singular hero items.

**Range: 2–5 slides per carousel.**

### COMPOSITION DISCIPLINE

- Hero dish CENTERED. Not thrown off-frame.
- No excessive empty space.
- No dead zones in diagonal splits — both halves have to earn their space.
- If a composition would require more than 1 regen to land, rethink the concept before spending credits.
