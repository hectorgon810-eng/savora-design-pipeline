## 1. BRAND WORLD STATEMENT

Savora is a restaurant-focused creative studio — photography, video, reels, social content, brand systems. We work with owner-operators who take pride in their food and want their marketing to match. Our clients' feeds look different after we arrive.

**The content goal: sell the work without selling.** Never a hard pitch. Every post is either (a) a piece of craft worth looking at, (b) a client result worth quoting, or (c) a window into how we make it. A potential client decides we're worth hiring because the work looks expensive, the process looks intentional, and the results are undeniable.

**Tension that makes Savora work:** premium craft ↔ approachable operator. We are a studio you can actually afford. We are Hector and a small team, not a faceless agency. The brand carries both weights — editorial-professional polish + handmade-local intimacy.

## 4. VOICE

**Understated, observant, confident. Never salesy, never hyperbolic.**

Allowed moves:
- *"Shot last Tuesday. First pour of the new menu."*
- *"Chef wanted it to feel like a Sunday in Oaxaca. We found the window."*
- *"Twenty-eight frames. This one earned its place."*
- *"Before Savora / After Savora."* (as carousel with real asset pair)
- *"The Blue Mezcal menu drops May 5. Here's the shoot that got us there."*

Forbidden:
- "Elevate your brand"
- "Take your restaurant to the next level"
- Emoji chains
- Generic agency-speak
- Fake urgency / fake scarcity
- Comparing to "other agencies"

## 5. CONTENT ENGINE — 10 POST TYPES

### 5.1 Hero Photograph (single image) — 20%
One great photo from a recent shoot. Minimal overlay — tiny Savora tag + client tag. The photo sells the service.
**Visual direction:** full-bleed client photo, bottom-strip small-caps "Shot for [Client Name]" + "Savora" mark.

### 5.2 Case Study — Before/After Carousel — 15%
5–7 slide carousel. Slide 1: opening photo (teaser). Slide 2: before (old asset). Slide 3: after (Savora asset). Slide 4–5: process / detail shots. Slide 6: metric card (real results). Slide 7: closing CTA.
**Requires:** real before/after imagery + real metrics per client.

### 5.3 Results Card / Metric Post — 10%
Editorial card: bold numeric stat, context, client tag.
**Examples:** *33,000 impressions · One post · @taqueriarojas* — treated as a gallery plaque not a brag.

### 5.4 Behind-the-Scenes (Process) — 10%
Photo of the shoot in progress. Camera cage, lightbox rig, chef at the pass, Hector framing the shot. Raw, documentary.
**Requires:** BTS shoots captured alongside client work. **[ASSET GAP]**

### 5.5 Testimonial Card — 10%
Real client quote, editorial treatment. Teal ground, italic serif pullquote, small-caps attribution.
**Requires:** collected quotes from Hope (Azteca), Jackson owner, Blue Mezcal ownership, Rojas.

### 5.6 Craft / Tool Still-Life — 8%
Single object from the studio: a camera body, a lens, the DaVinci timeline in color, a printed moodboard, a Pantone book.
**Requires:** tool photography session. **[ASSET GAP]**

### 5.7 Brand-World Reveal (Meta-Editorial) — 8%
Show the SYSTEM: a brand-world doc page, a moodboard carousel, a palette card, the wireframe of a client site.
**Requires:** screenshot / mock of internal assets (this pipeline, DESIGN_SYSTEM docs, site wireframes).

### 5.8 Founder / Team Portrait — 7%
Hector at work. Behind the camera. At the edit desk. On location. Portra grain, warm documentary.
**Requires:** founder portrait session. **[ASSET GAP]**

### 5.9 Service-Explainer Editorial — 7%
Type-first card: "What a Savora package looks like" — breakdown of what's included without dollar amounts. Editorial, not salesy.
**Requires:** service-tier copy locked (T1/T2/T3 already logged).

### 5.10 Philosophy / Manifesto — 5%
Typography-only post. A single line of conviction.
**Examples:**
- *"Restaurants should own the story. Not the algorithm."*
- *"The food did the work. We just framed it."*
- *"Good marketing photographs what is, not what could be."*

## 6. VISUAL LANGUAGE

### 6.1 Palette (Savora known)
- **Deep teal** `#0F3B3C` — primary ink
- **Burnt orange** `#D5662E` — accent (rare, reserved for CTAs + kickers)
- **Warm cream** `#F4ECD8` — ground
- **Charcoal / off-black** `#17140F` — anchor ink / shadow
- **Metallic gold / brass** `#C9A24B` — occasional foil accent on case-study cards (earned only)

**Dominance:** 60 cream · 30 teal · 10 accent (burnt orange OR brass).

### 6.2 Typography
- **Hero display** — refined editorial serif (Fraunces / Canela / GT Sectra italic). Teal on cream. Small-caps for labels.
- **Sans labels** — Geist, Söhne, or Neue Haas Grotesk. Wide-tracked small-caps.
- **Mono** — Geist Mono or JetBrains Mono for metric cards (tabular numerals for stats).

### 6.3 Composition
- Editorial-restraint default. 60% negative space.
- Case-study carousels use a consistent grid locked at the campaign start.
- Full-bleed client photography when the photo can carry it.

### 6.4 Photography posture
- **Savora-shot client work** (primary content source): Portra 400/800 grain, warm tungsten or editorial daylight, shallow DoF, honest-documentary or editorial-still-life depending on client register.
- **Studio BTS**: 35mm, documentary, warm tungsten, slight motion blur OK. **[ASSET GAP]**
- **Founder portraits**: Portra 800, tungsten 2800K, in-action not posed. **[ASSET GAP]**

### 6.5 Graphic elements
- Hairline teal rules (section dividers)
- Small-caps tracked-wide labels
- Circular "Shot by Savora" seal (brass hairline, used once per post max)
- Metric card treatment: tabular numerals + small-caps label

## 9. CAMPAIGN STRUCTURES

### A. Client Case Study Drop (7-slide carousel)
1. Opening hero photo (best shot from project)
2. Context: client + scope (small-caps editorial card)
3. Before / after visual pair
4. Process BTS detail
5. Detail shot (a favorite single frame)
6. Metric card (real numbers)
7. Testimonial quote (if available) OR "We built this for [Client] · If you'd like something similar, [CTA]"

### B. Service Week (3-post rhythm)
- Post 1 (Mon): hero photo tagged "photography"
- Post 2 (Wed): reel tagged "video"
- Post 3 (Fri): meta-editorial tagged "brand systems"

### C. Founder Series (monthly)
One post per month: Hector shooting / editing / reviewing work. Portra grain, voice pull-quote, tied back to a specific client project.

## 10. COHESION RULE

Every Savora post must show at least TWO of:
1. **Editorial typography** — refined serif or precision small-caps
2. **Teal + cream palette dominance** — 60/30 rule
3. **Client work credited** OR **Savora process shown** — every post earns its place by being either portfolio or proof

The one-second test: a potential client scrolls past — in one second they should register "restaurant photography studio" without reading a word. Palette + typography carry it.
