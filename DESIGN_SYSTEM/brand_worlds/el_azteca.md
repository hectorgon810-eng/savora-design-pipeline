# El Azteca — Brand World

*A living design system, not a template set. El Azteca should feel like a family restaurant whose graphic design is run by a zine studio in Mexico City.*

Version: v1 — 2026-04-22
Status: draft (awaiting Hector review)

---

## 1. BRAND WORLD STATEMENT

El Azteca is **a playful-maximalist family Mexican restaurant with craft-design discipline.** La Bamba Ice Cream × Brain Dead × Oaxacan calendar art × a Sunday comida with tía. The food is generous, the energy is celebratory, the graphic identity is a riot of color held in check by real typographic discipline.

Every graphic feels hand-mixed, paper-stock, poster-printed. Never digital-Canva, never cheap stock. The warrior logo stays full-color in a corner. The palette is PASTEL-SATURATED — not flat-saturated. The design feels like it could have been printed by a Oaxacan screen-printer who moonlights for Supreme.

**The tension that makes Azteca work:** maximalist chaos ↔ craft discipline. Color + halftone + hand-drawn arrows + papel picado accents — but every element is intentional. Nothing is random, nothing is cheap, nothing is templated.

Where Jackson House is heritage-luxe with a wink, and Blue Mezcal is bold-editorial cocktail bar, El Azteca is **the family-celebration brand** — the one a multigenerational table comes to for Sunday lunch, the one the kids love, the one that still has to feel designed enough that a twenty-five-year-old design-literate diner also posts it to their story.

## 2. CREATIVE DIRECTION — THE WORLD IT LIVES IN

**If it was a fashion brand:** Brain Dead × Online Ceramics × Stüssy × an Oaxacan bordado collective. Saturated colors, hand-drawn graphics, heritage textiles treated with skate-brand attitude.

**If it was a magazine:** *Pin-Up* × *Apartamento* × a Oaxacan zine called *Maíz*. Photo-driven, unpretentious, full of personality, occasional surprise collage.

**If it was a physical space beyond the restaurant:** a Oaxacan street market on a Saturday at 11am — papel picado overhead, a hand-painted sign above every stall, pink-and-turquoise stucco walls, a warrior figurine on the counter, a grandma making tortillas in the corner, kids running around.

**If it was a film:** *Coco* × *Y Tu Mamá También* × *Roma* — celebration, family, specific Mexican cultural warmth, NOT generic "fiesta" Disneyfication.

**If it was a song:** Rigo Tovar cumbia covered by a young band from Guadalajara who also follows Brain Dead on Instagram.

## 3. THE TENSIONS (what makes it NOT just one thing)

| Tension | How it shows up | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Maximalist chaos ↔ craft discipline | Color riot + clean typography | Designed, not loud-for-loud's-sake |
| Family warmth ↔ streetwear cool | Warrior logo + halftone overlay + Cooper Black | Serves three generations |

## 4. VOICE

**Family-warm, bilingual Spanish-primary, celebration energy. Never ironic-detached, never celebration-for-celebration's-sake.**

**Food voice** — celebratory-familiar. The food is how the family loves you.
- *"Guacamole, but make it octopus."*
- *"Not your average appetizer."*
- *"We don't do things halfway."*
- *"Classic for a reason."*
- *"De la casa."*
- *"Auténtico."*

**Promo / event voice** — warm, inviting, never hard-sell.
- *"Taco Tuesday. Since forever."*
- *"Happy Hour · Pick your poison. 4-6pm."*
- *"Come hungry. Leave happy."*

**Utility voice** — warm-direct, bilingual if the audience is.
- *"Ahora en DoorDash. / Now on DoorDash."*
- *"¡Con gusto! Reservations open for Mother's Day."*

**Bilingual rule:** Spanish is PRIMARY on headline. English italic beneath when needed. Cultural holidays (Día de Muertos, Independence Day) go Spanish-only. Avoid tokenism — Spanish phrases used must make sense to someone who speaks Spanish, never awkward.

**Forbidden patterns:** no "olé!" (tourist Spanish), no exclamation-point chains, no smart-mouth JH lines ("You already know"), no BM-style poetic-moody dichos (wrong voice), no generic restaurant-marketing clichés.

## 5. CONTENT ENGINE — 10 POST TYPES

### 5.1 Hero Dish (Arrow-Labeled) — *25% of feed*
- **Purpose:** signature AZ post — hero dish with hand-drawn semantic arrows pointing to ingredients
- **Visual direction:** pastel-saturated color field (pink, turquoise, cream, or chile-red), photo plated 3/4 or overhead, 3-5 hand-drawn marker arrows pointing to named ingredients, each arrow with 2-4 Spanish-primary word label
- **Trigger:** any new or signature dish
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** playful-maximalist family

### 5.2 Hero Dish (Clean Editorial) — *12% of feed*
- **Purpose:** alternative to arrow-labeled — let the photo shine, no arrows. Warrior logo corner only.
- **Visual direction:** pastel-saturated ground, photo 60%+ canvas, Cooper Black or chunky serif headline, warrior logo corner, halftone texture background only
- **Trigger:** when dish photo is strong enough to carry alone, recurring signatures
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** playful-maximalist

### 5.3 Drink / Cocktail Spotlight — *10% of feed*
- **Purpose:** margaritas, micheladas, aguas frescas, mexican imports
- **Visual direction:** pastel-saturated ground, glass photo, chunky playful caps, optional citrus/lime wedge hand-drawn ornament, halftone overlay
- **Trigger:** new drinks, happy hour features
- **Aspect:** 4:5

### 5.4 Promo / Flyer (Event + Holiday) — *15% of feed*
- **Purpose:** Taco Tuesday, happy hour, holidays (Independence Day, Día de Muertos, Valentine's), cumbia nights
- **Visual direction:** full flyer treatment — masthead headline in chunky caps, photo or illustrated hero, papel-picado accent corners (sparingly), small-caps date/time/price, warrior logo. This is the LOUDEST post type — let it be.
- **Trigger:** event calendar
- **Aspect:** 4:5 or 1:1
- **Register:** playful-maximalist

### 5.5 Typography-First / Dicho Post — *8% of feed*
- **Purpose:** Spanish dichos ("La comida es amor"), brand philosophy, single loud headline post, no photo
- **Visual direction:** pastel-saturated single-color field, chunky serif caps or hand-painted brush script, 5-8% halftone texture, hairline frame optional
- **Trigger:** cultural moments, "Why we cook" voice posts, dichos
- **Aspect:** 1:1 or 4:5

### 5.6 Culture / Family / Behind-the-Scenes — *10% of feed*
- **Purpose:** kitchen crew, abuela's recipe, tortilla-making, family portraits, Sunday service
- **Visual direction:** documentary 35mm warm daylight, Portra 400 grain, subject in action, subtle overlay of handwritten annotation if earned
- **Trigger:** monthly features, holiday family stories
- **Aspect:** 4:5

### 5.7 Location / Map Story — *5% of feed*
- **Purpose:** Delaware + Rehoboth positioning, bring the multiple locations into the brand
- **Visual direction:** **riso-printed, loteria-card-inspired stylized map** — hand-illustrated cartoon map of Delaware with both locations marked as colorful landmarks. Pink + turquoise + chile-red + marigold ink. Warrior icon + chile icon + corn icon as decorative waypoints. Handwritten "¡Estamos aquí!" callouts. Optional: unreal-engine style cartoon render with papel-picado banner overhead — AZ CAN carry the more maximalist stylized-map direction.
- **Trigger:** cross-location promo, new-location openings, "find us" posts
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** playful-maximalist

### 5.8 Utility / Operational — *5% of feed*
- **Purpose:** DoorDash, hours, reservations, holiday closures
- **Visual direction:** DESIGNED. Pastel-saturated ground, chunky headline, warrior logo corner, halftone background. Never plain. Bilingual where appropriate.
- **Trigger:** operational info
- **Aspect:** 4:5 or 1:1
- **Rule:** utility posts get the same craft love as hero posts. Tacombi's "Combi Club" flyer is the reference — a utility announcement treated as full graphic design.

### 5.9 Cross-Brand / Azteca Location Post — *5% of feed*
- **Purpose:** content shared between multiple AZ locations AND BM (Millionaire Margarita when branded Azteca), per cross-post rules
- **Visual direction:** standard AZ treatment
- **Trigger:** shared promotional content
- **Aspect:** 4:5

### 5.10 Campaign Drop — *5% of feed (used in 3-5 post sequences)*
- **Purpose:** themed multi-post campaigns
- **Visual direction:** see §9 Campaign Structure
- **Trigger:** new menu launches, cultural holidays, partnerships

## 6. VISUAL LANGUAGE

### 6.1 Typography — behavior, not just fonts

**Primary hierarchy:**
- **Hero display** — bold chunky display: Cooper Black, Sharp Grotesk Ultra, Obviously. Clean and readable — no grain, no distress, no knockout ON the letterforms.
- **Hand-painted brush accent** — for cultural / dicho posts. Used sparingly as accent word.
- **Body / labels** — friendly rounded sans or small-caps Trade Gothic. Bilingual-ready.
- **Script** — hand-drawn script only, for cheerful "¡Estamos aquí!" / "Thanks y'all!" moments. Never formal script.

**Behavior rules:**
- Letterforms STAY CLEAN. Texture lives in background FIELDS, never on the type.
- Address / award / tiny bottom copy is at least 18-20pt equivalent — NEVER micro-decorative-print.
- Headlines crisp. Body readable.
- NO Trajan, NO Papyrus, NO generic Mexican-restaurant fonts

### 6.2 Color usage — the Azteca riot palette

**Full palette:**
- Hot pink `#FF5BA7`
- Sunshine yellow `#FFD400`
- Turquoise `#1FB5B0`
- Chile red `#E63946`
- Masa cream `#F8F2DF`
- Cobalt `#1A3FA8` (heritage accent)

**Pairing rules (from Azteca learning log):**

**Pairings that WORK:**
- Pink + red + cream (warm, family-celebration)
- Blue + red (complementary for red-dominant food)
- Pink + cream (soft-playful)
- Turquoise + yellow + cream (vibrant-celebration)
- Pink + yellow (high-energy)

**Pairings to AVOID:**
- Black + yellow (harsh school-bus)
- Blue + black (flat)
- Mono-saturated flat anything (reads cheap)
- More than 4 palette colors in one layout (too busy)

**Lean PASTEL-SATURATED, not flat-saturated — colors feel hand-mixed, paper-stock, poster-printed, not digital Canva.**

### 6.3 Composition DNA

- Full-field saturated color ground
- Photo occupies 50-65% of canvas
- Halftone dots at 45° on backgrounds OK
- Subtle risograph misregistration OK (1-3px color-channel offset)
- Slight off-axis rotation of stickered elements OK (±3-8°)
- Less texture overall than earlier iterations — family-restaurant warmth, not zine-overload
- Warrior logo corner placement 80-120px full-color
- NEVER diagonal slashes or horizontal bands that CUT THROUGH the food photo

### 6.4 Image treatment

**Photography:**
- Daylight-bright, slightly oversaturated, warm Portra 400 grain
- Overhead flat-lay or three-quarter angle
- 5500K natural daylight
- Soft shadow upper-right
- Hand-made, family, warm

**Arrow treatment (AZ signature):**
- Hand-drawn marker arrows, 2-3px stroke with slight hand-wobble
- EVERY arrow must POINT AT the specific named ingredient visible in the photo (shrimp → shrimp, salsa → sauce pool, aguacate → avocado)
- 3-5 arrows max per layout
- Label text 2-4 words Spanish-primary
- Arrows at random sauce pools, rim, empty space = WRONG

**Photo-to-field transition:**
- 60-120px soft gradient dissolve where color field meets photo edge
- NEVER a hard knife-edge
- No bands cutting through the food

**Real-image identity lock applies always.**

### 6.5 Graphic elements — ornament library

**AZ-world library:**
- Warrior logo (mandatory corner placement on most posts, 80-120px full color)
- Chile silhouette
- Corn cob outline
- Tortilla-press mark
- Skull / Day-of-Dead hairline
- Warrior-shield mini-mark
- Hand-drawn star-burst
- Sun-ray hairline
- Lime-wedge cross-section
- Papel picado corner cutout (small, accent only)
- Heart motif (for Valentine's / family posts)

**Forbidden:**
- Agave leaf (that's BM)
- Wheat / butter / flame / skillet (that's JH)
- Neon-club typography
- Cursive-wedding scripts
- Generic "fiesta" clip-art (stock) papel picado
- Margarita-glass-with-lime clipart

**Textures:**
- Halftone dots 45° — background fields OK
- Risograph misregistration — subtle, 1-3px
- Subtle paper-stock grain (linen, cream laid paper)
- NEVER drop shadows — this register is flat-layered playful-maximalist graphic, not dimensional. Dimension comes from layered fields + halftone + arrows.

Target mix across any 10-post window:
- **40% structured** — Hero Dish (arrow-labeled) + Hero Dish (clean) + Drink Spotlight in standard layouts
- **20% promo / flyer** — events, holidays, happy hour
- **15% campaign** — part of a sequence

## 9. CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE — 3-5 post sequences

**Pattern A — Seasonal Menu Launch (4 posts):**
1. Masthead reveal — "Nuevo Menú · Octubre" in chunky caps on pink ground, no photo
2. Hero dish (arrow-labeled) — the star of the menu
3. Drink pairing spotlight
4. Family / behind-the-scenes reel of prep

**Pattern B — Cultural Holiday (3 posts — Día de Muertos):**
1. Altar / ofrenda editorial photo, Spanish-primary copy, English italic beneath
2. A dish on the ofrenda — hero shot with cultural reverence
3. A dicho typography-first post in chile-red + cream, hand-painted brush accent

**Pattern C — Cross-Location Campaign (3 posts):**
1. Map story — both locations marked on a loteria-style stylized map
2. Delaware location hero dish
3. Rehoboth location hero dish

**Pattern D — Taco Tuesday Recurring (weekly, single post):**
- Every Tuesday, a Taco Tuesday post — rotating dish, rotating layout, but the "TACO TUESDAY" masthead is locked. Builds recognition over time.

## 10. COHESION RULE — the ONE thing that makes every post recognizable as Azteca

**The AZ cohesion signature is three things, at least two of which must appear in every post:**

1. **Pastel-saturated field color** (pink, turquoise, yellow, chile-red, masa-cream, or cobalt as ground) — never flat digital, always with subtle halftone or paper grain.
2. **Warrior logo corner placement** — 80-120px full color, one of four corners, recognizable silhouette.
3. **Clean chunky display typography** (Cooper Black or similar family) — bold, readable, never distressed ON the type.

The one-second test: AZ in a feed next to Taquero Mucho and Tacombi should hold its own — identifiably AZ by the warrior + palette + typography.

## 12. SEASONAL / OCCASIONAL OVERRIDES

- **Día de Muertos (Nov 1-2):** Spanish primary, English italic beneath. Marigold + chile-red + papel picado accents. Ofrenda imagery.
- **Mexican Independence Day (Sept 15-16):** green + white + red accents, cultural reverence
- **Cinco de Mayo:** acknowledge but don't lead with it (it's more American than Mexican). Favor authentic holidays.
- **Valentine's Day:** pink-dominant palette, paired-food imagery, heart motif OK
- **Christmas / New Year:** family-gathering imagery, warmer palette, tamales / ponche features
- **Mother's / Father's Day:** family portraits, reservation-driven promo
