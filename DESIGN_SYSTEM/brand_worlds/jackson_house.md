# Jackson House — Brand World

*A living design system, not a template set. Jackson House should feel like a 1920s oak-paneled chophouse whose bartenders all went to art school.*

Version: v1 — 2026-04-22
Status: draft (awaiting Hector review)

---

## 1. BRAND WORLD STATEMENT

Jackson House is **a dual-personality restaurant**. The food side is *Delaware Today 2025 Best New Restaurant* — chef-driven, heritage-luxe, Frenchette meets Gage & Tollner meets an old-school American chophouse. The cocktail side is **Selena's universe** — every drink has a name that is a pun, a song lyric, a double-entendre, or a cultural wink (Heard's The Last Word, Bite The Bulleit, One In A Melon, Grapefruitty Tuti, Nada De Mango, Spritz From Switz, Key Lime Mule, Toast Of The Town, La Ultima).

Older regulars treat JH like their Monday-night supper club. Travelers come in specifically for the food (Bison Ribeye, Passionfruit Salmon, Shrimp & Lobster Scampi, Elote Burger, Orecchiette). The staff is young — servers, bar team, back of house. Their energy is what keeps the place from tipping into "stuffy steakhouse." The visual identity has to honor the heritage without mummifying it.

**The tension that makes JH work:** brass-foil reverence ↔ wordplay-cheek. The food is plated with a straight face. The cocktail menu is laughing. Both are designed with equal craft.

This is not farm-table rustic. This is not tavern-casual. This is **foil-stamped heritage with a young heart.** Think: what if Drakes London opened a restaurant and hired a twenty-eight-year-old head bartender who reads poetry.

## 2. CREATIVE DIRECTION — THE WORLD IT LIVES IN

**If it was a fashion brand:** Todd Snyder × Drakes London × a bespoke tailor from Jermyn Street. Oxblood, brass, oak, charcoal wool. Traditional silhouettes, contemporary cuts.

**If it was a magazine:** *Monocle* × *The Gentleman's Journal* × *Esquire* circa 1962, with a cocktail column written by someone from *The Cut*.

**If it was a physical space beyond the restaurant:** a members' club in Philadelphia — oak paneling, brass fittings, tufted leather, green banker's lamps, candles on every table, Maker's Mark bottle on every bar back, a 1940s menu card framed near the host stand, and a playlist curated by someone who also DJs at Public Records.

**If it was a film:** *Mad Men* × *The Menu* × *Chef's Table* — executive-suite weight + culinary seriousness + occasional wry humor.

**If it was a song:** Frank Sinatra b-side covered by Leon Bridges.

## 3. THE TENSIONS (what makes it NOT just one thing)

| Tension | How it shows up | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Heritage food ↔ wordplay cocktails | Food = straight face, drinks = pun headlines | Two voices, one brand |
| Older regulars ↔ young staff ↔ traveler destination | Multigenerational photos in team posts | The brand isn't for one age, it's for one taste level |
| Brass-foil luxe ↔ cocktail-menu cheek | Foil-stamp type on black for food, script+serif pun lockups for drinks | Keeps the design system interesting |
| Chef seriousness ↔ bartender wit | Chef = reverent documentary; Bartender = smirk | Visual tonal shift, never voice-confused |
| Delaware ↔ New York plate | Middletown address, Manhattan cooking | Small-town warmth + city ambition |

## 4. VOICE

**One brand, THREE sharply distinct voices depending on what's being posted.**

**Food voice** — classical, warm, telegraphic. Let the plate do the work. Never explain unless the dish is new.
- *"You already know."*
- *"Some things don't need to be complicated."*
- *"Warm out of the oven. Butter melts on contact."*
- *"Dry-aged 28 days. That's the whole sentence."*

**Cocktail voice (Selena-mode)** — wordplay, punny, confident. Earns its cheek because the drink is actually good. The name IS the headline.
- *"Bite The Bulleit."*
- *"Nada De Mango. Everything to Say."*
- *"Heard's The Last Word. (It usually is.)"*
- *"Toast Of The Town. Take a bite."*
- *"One In A Melon. (She said it, not us.)"*

**Utility voice** — warm, adult, zero-degradation. Operational info still designed and voiced with taste.
- *"Reservations open for Mother's Day. Don't wait."*
- *"Now delivering through DoorDash. The Elote Burger travels well."*
- *"Closed Christmas Day. Back with steak the 26th."*

**Forbidden patterns:** no exclamation points (except in cocktail names that earn one), no generic foodie emoji chains, no "tag someone," no "comment below," no Spanish phrases (wrong brand — that's BM/AZ), no dad-jokes (the cocktail names walk that line already, don't push it further).

**The Selena tell:** if a cocktail post's headline is the drink name AND that name is a pun or wordplay, the supporting line can be a single in-joke or nothing. Trust the name to carry it.

## 5. CONTENT ENGINE — 10 POST TYPES

### 5.1 Hero Dish Editorial — *22% of feed*
- **Purpose:** showcase one dish, Frenchette / Monocle reverence
- **Visual direction:** tight 3/4 or overhead, dark moody lighting, brass-foil serif caps or script+serif lockup, deep black or oxblood ground
- **Trigger:** new menu item, chef's special, signature dish repost
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** heritage-tavern

### 5.2 Cocktail Spotlight (Selena Series) — *20% of feed*
- **Purpose:** showcase one drink, pun-forward headline, make Selena the recurring face of the cocktail program
- **Visual direction:** script + serif caps lockup with the drink NAME as the hero (Frenchette move). Warm cream or deep charcoal ground. Glass rim-lit. Single brand-accent color (brass, oxblood, or forest green, NOT all three).
- **Trigger:** new cocktail, menu rotation, Monday-night feature
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** heritage-tavern but with script+serif playfulness

### 5.3 Chef / Bartender Personality — *12% of feed*
- **Purpose:** humanize the kitchen and the bar. Selena gets her own recurring frame. Chef gets documentary respect.
- **Visual direction:** Portra 800 grain, tungsten 2800K, subject in action (plating, shaking, pouring). Small-caps name + role caption. Optional: pull-quote in script italic.
- **Trigger:** monthly team-member feature, new-hire introduction, event coverage, Selena's "drink of the week" vlog-style still
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** heritage-tavern

### 5.4 Menu / Carousel Editorial — *8% of feed*
- **Purpose:** show range — multi-item carousel. Editorial magazine pages.
- **Visual direction:** 5-7 slide carousel, each slide treated as a page in a cocktail/dinner menu. Brass hairline section dividers. Small-caps category labels. Photo-plate-per-page.
- **Trigger:** seasonal menu drop, new happy hour menu, private-dining menu
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** heritage-tavern

### 5.5 Typography-First Post (Drink-Name Masthead) — *10% of feed*
- **Purpose:** let Selena's wordplay carry a whole post alone. No photo needed.
- **Visual direction:** full-canvas script+serif lockup of the drink name. Oxblood or deep charcoal ground. Brass-foil hairline rule. Small italic venue tag at bottom.
- **Trigger:** cocktail-menu launch, single-drink feature, "drink of the week" typographic
- **Aspect:** 4:5 or 1:1
- **Register:** heritage-tavern with script-lockup emphasis

### 5.6 Promo / Event Flyer — *8% of feed*
- **Purpose:** announce events — live music nights, wine dinners, private parties, holiday reservations
- **Visual direction:** poster-level design. Masthead top, hero photo OR illustration middle, small-caps date/time/price lockup bottom. Brass hairline dividers.
- **Trigger:** event calendar entries, reservation drives, holiday specials
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** heritage-tavern

### 5.7 Location / Map Story — *4% of feed*
- **Purpose:** Middletown DE positioning — make JH the landmark, not just an address
- **Visual direction:** **etched line-art / engraving style map** (Alfred reference) — black ink on warm cream paper, Middletown rendered as a vintage Saturday Evening Post illustration. JH marked as the prominent hand-drawn building. Surrounding blocks shown as hairline architectural silhouettes. Optional: small compass rose, scale bar, and a faux-vintage "Est. [year]" line.
- **Rendering style:** engraved illustration, NOT photorealistic, NOT unreal-engine (that's AZ). Think vintage Gage & Tollner menu illustration.
- **Trigger:** grand-opening anniversaries, "first-time visitor" post, traveler-destination positioning, neighborhood cross-promo
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** heritage-tavern

### 5.8 Utility / Operational — *8% of feed*
- **Purpose:** DoorDash, hours, reservations, holiday, patio-now-open
- **Visual direction:** designed as a poster, NEVER plain text. Brass-foil headline on deep ground. Small-caps dateline. Warm cream body copy. Hairline rule footer.
- **Trigger:** operational info
- **Aspect:** 4:5 or 1:1
- **Register:** heritage-tavern
- **Rule:** utility posts get the SAME foil-stamped treatment as hero posts. No degradation. If it's worth posting, it's worth designing.

### 5.9 Cultural / Personality Post — *5% of feed*
- **Purpose:** brand-voice pieces that aren't a dish or drink. Alfred's "Our Commitment to Quality" pattern. Chef's philosophy, the history of a technique, why the bison ribeye is 18oz.
- **Visual direction:** italic serif headline top, small engraved illustration middle (chef's tools, a wheat sheaf, the building facade), body paragraph below in warm cream on charcoal. Hairline frame.
- **Trigger:** once-monthly brand-story drop, anniversary posts, "behind the hospitality" moments
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** heritage-tavern

### 5.10 Campaign Drop — *3% of feed (used in 3-5 post sequences)*
- **Purpose:** a themed multi-post campaign
- **Visual direction:** see §9 Campaign Structure
- **Trigger:** menu-launch, seasonal, Delaware Today anniversary, chef's-table residency

## 6. VISUAL LANGUAGE

### 6.1 Typography — behavior, not just fonts

**Primary hierarchy:**
- **Hero display (food mode)** — old-style serif: Farnham, Sentinel, Caponi, Caslon. All-caps or tightly-tracked small-caps. Rendered AS metallic brass foil with 3-stop gradient + specular highlight.
- **Hero display (cocktail mode / Selena)** — **script + serif dual-weight lockup**. The drink name splits: script italic for connecting words ("The," "Of," "In"), serif caps for power words ("Last Word," "Melon," "Bulleit"). Frenchette move.
- **Subhead / label** — condensed all-caps grotesque: Knockout, Trade Gothic Condensed. Warm cream on dark ground. Wide tracking (+40 to +120 em).
- **Body** — Trade Gothic or warm serif (Caslon text-weight), 28-36px, leading 1.3-1.45.
- **Italic pull-quote** — Caslon Italic or Farnham Italic, script accent only, one line.

**Behavior rules:**
- Brass-foil gradient type is mandatory on every hero food post (it IS the recognition signature)
- Script+serif dual-weight lockup is mandatory on every Selena cocktail post
- NO Trajan, NO generic "steakhouse" fonts, NO Herr Von Muellerhoff, NO overused wedding calligraphy scripts
- NO drop shadows on brass-foil type (foil has its own dimension via gradient)

### 6.2 Color usage — strict palette

**Primary palette:**
- Charred black `#0A0A0A` — primary ground
- Deep charcoal `#1A1612` — ground gradient partner
- Brass `#C9A24B` → antique gold `#B08937` → brass `#C9A24B` — hero type foil gradient
- Warm cream `#F2EADF` — body type + accent rules
- Forest green `#1F3B2D` — deep tonal gradient field (never flat)
- Oxblood `#5B1A1A` — deep tonal gradient field (never flat)
- Mustard `#C8962F` — rare accent only

**Rules:**
- 60 black/charcoal ground, 30 brass, 10 cream
- Forest green and oxblood are ALTERNATIVE grounds — pick one ground per post, never mix
- Brass is ALWAYS a gradient, never a flat hex fill
- Mustard is accent only, under 5% of canvas
- NEVER use cobalt (that's BM), NEVER use hot pink/turquoise (that's AZ)

### 6.3 Composition DNA

- Dark-ground dominant (cream-ground OK for cultural / map posts only)
- 40-60% canvas fill — tavern editorial restraint, never sparse, never packed
- Hairline brass rules separating sections (2-3 per layout OK)
- Asymmetric layouts > centered menu-cards
- Script+serif lockup can be centered (ceremonial) or lower-left (casual) depending on register
- Minimum canvas edge margin 40px on 1080
- NEVER bisect the hero photo with a color band

### 6.4 Image treatment

**Photography:**
- Portra 800 grain emulation mandatory
- Tungsten 2800-3000K (candlelight / warm interior) for most food + cocktail
- Shallow depth of field, rich midtones, deep blacks
- Comfort-food plated generously but photographed with editorial discipline
- Chef portraits: documentary, in-action, not posed. Hands in frame, steam, flame.

**Illustrations (for maps, cultural posts, menu dividers):**
- Etched / engraved line art, black ink on cream ground
- Hairline weight (1-2px equivalent)
- Scalable — can be a small corner ornament or a full hero
- Think: vintage menu card, 1940s restaurant brochure, Alfred's "Our Commitment to Quality" illustration register

**Real-image identity lock applies always** — never regenerate the dish photo.

### 6.5 Graphic elements — ornament library

**JH-world library (optional, ONE per layout max, default zero):**
- Wheat sheaf silhouette (brass-foil hairline OR inked cream hairline)
- Rolling pin outline
- Cast-iron skillet silhouette
- Fork-and-knife cross
- Butter-pat silhouette
- Flame curl (single small flame, used for "hot out of the oven" posts)
- Olive-sprig hairline
- Wine-glass silhouette
- Coffee-bean outline
- Brass circular seal (for award / anniversary posts)
- Hairline brass rule / underline

**Forbidden:**
- Agave leaf (that's BM)
- Warrior / chile / corn / skull (that's AZ)
- Papel picado (wrong register)
- Cheap neon outlines
- Photoshop default lens flare
- Cursive-font flourishes that look like wedding invitations

**Textures:**
- 5-8% noise grain overlay on every flat field
- Subtle paper-grain impression UNDER brass-foil type (simulates foil on bookbinding paper)
- Portra 800 film grain on photography
- Faint edge vignette OK on nocturnal photography
- Forest-green and oxblood fields ALWAYS have 5-12% tonal gradient

## 8. VARIATION LOGIC

Target mix across any 10-post window:
- **45% structured** — Hero Dish + Selena Cocktail in standard layouts
- **20% experimental** — Typography-First, Oversized-type, Recipe-sidebar equivalents
- **15% campaign** — part of a sequence
- **10% utility** — DoorDash, hours, reservations — DESIGNED
- **5% personality / cultural** — team features, brand-voice posts
- **5% disruptive** — rule-breaking (e.g. all-cream-ground post, illustration-only post, all-script-type post)

## 9. CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE — 3-5 post sequences

**Pattern A — New Cocktail Launch with Selena (4 posts):**
1. **Wordplay teaser** — typography-first post, full-canvas script+serif lockup of the new drink name. No photo. Oxblood ground. *"NADA DE MANGO · coming 4.28"*
2. **Reveal** — hero cocktail spotlight. Glass rim-lit, Selena's hand pouring in frame if practical.
3. **Ingredient build** — labeled-diagram post (hairline callouts) showing the ingredients. Educational respect.
4. **Selena at the bar** — portrait post of Selena, script pull-quote like *"It took me three tries. Worth it."*

**Pattern B — Menu Drop Campaign (5 posts, 2 weeks):**
1. Masthead reveal — "NEW MENU · MAY 5" in brass-foil caps, no photo
2. Hero food shot (#1 signature dish)
3. Hero Selena cocktail shot (#1 signature drink)
4. Chef + Selena at the pass reel / documentary post
5. Menu carousel — 5-6 slides, editorial pages

**Pattern C — Traveler Destination / Anniversary (3 posts):**
1. Map / engraved illustration post: "Find us in Middletown"
2. A traveler-review typographic quote post
3. A cultural / brand-voice Alfred-style "why we built this" post

**Pattern D — Selena Series (recurring, not a finite campaign):**
- Every 8-10 posts, a Selena post — either a new cocktail, a bar-back portrait, or a drink-name typography post. The recurrence builds her as a brand character over time.

## 10. COHESION RULE — the ONE thing that makes every post recognizable as Jackson House

**The JH cohesion signature is three things, at least two of which must appear in every post:**

1. **Brass-foil type gradient OR script+serif dual-weight lockup** — one of these two type treatments is the recognition signal.
2. **Deep grounded palette** — charred black, deep charcoal, oxblood, or forest green as dominant ground. Warm cream accents.
3. **Editorial composition discipline** — hairline rules, small-caps labels, 40-60% fill, no cheap effects.

The one-second test: JH in a feed next to Frenchette and Gage & Tollner should be indistinguishable from them in quality, identifiable as distinct by palette.

## 11. CONTENT-TYPE-SPECIFIC RULES

### 11.1 Hero Dish Editorial
- Brass-foil caps MANDATORY
- Photo 55% canvas min
- Dark-ground default
- Copy: dish name verbatim (option a) OR telegraphic one-liner

### 11.2 Cocktail Spotlight (Selena Series)
- Script+serif dual-weight lockup of drink name = hero
- Drink name verbatim — Selena's puns are the voice
- Support line: zero or one in-joke. Trust the name.
- Brass foil OK but script dominates — this is where script earns its place
- Selena's hand / Selena herself in frame when practical

### 11.3 Chef / Bartender Personality
- Portra 800 grain mandatory
- Subject in action
- Small-caps name + role caption
- Pull-quote in Caslon Italic if quoted
- Separate Selena posts (script-heavy) from Chef posts (brass-foil-heavy)

### 11.4 Menu / Carousel Editorial
- 5-7 slides
- Slide 1: masthead
- Slides 2-6: one dish or drink per slide, editorial-page layout
- Slide 7: small-caps address / CTA / reservation link

### 11.5 Typography-First Post (Drink-Name Masthead)
- NO photo
- Full-canvas script+serif lockup
- Oxblood or charcoal ground
- Brass hairline rule + small italic venue tag

### 11.6 Promo / Event Flyer
- Masthead top
- Hero image OR engraved illustration middle
- Small-caps info lockup bottom
- Brass hairline dividers

### 11.7 Location / Map Story
- Etched / engraving line-art illustration
- Black ink on warm cream ground
- JH as prominent hand-drawn building landmark
- Middletown neighborhood as hairline silhouettes around it
- Optional: compass rose, scale bar, "Est." line
- NOT photorealistic, NOT unreal-engine
- Approved stylistic anchor: Alfred "Our Commitment to Quality" illustration register, or vintage Gage & Tollner menu card

### 11.8 Utility / Operational
- SAME visual treatment as hero posts
- Brass-foil headline, deep ground, small-caps dateline, hairline footer
- Voice: warm, adult, zero exclamation points

### 11.9 Cultural / Personality Post
- Italic serif headline top (Farnham Italic)
- Small engraved illustration middle
- Body paragraph warm cream on charcoal
- Hairline frame or 5-8% paper-grain texture border

### 11.10 Campaign Drop
- Visual thread locked at campaign start
- Each post evolves the thread
- Campaign closes with call-back to post #1
