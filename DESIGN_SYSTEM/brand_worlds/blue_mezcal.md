# Blue Mezcal — Brand World

*A living design system, not a template set. The Blue Mezcal feed should feel run by a creative agency that also happens to love mezcal.*

Version: v1 — 2026-04-22
Status: draft (awaiting Hector review)

---

## 1. BRAND WORLD STATEMENT

Blue Mezcal is **the bold sibling of Azteca** — a neighborhood agave bar that is also the area's best modern Mexican kitchen. Loud on Friday night, candlelit on Tuesday. Where Pujol's plating sensibility meets Handshake Speakeasy's crowd. The food is serious. The bar is a party. Both are treated with equal design reverence.

It is *not* a quiet library. It is *not* Céline-minimal. It is a **social, cocktail-forward, elevated-Mexican bar-and-kitchen** that carries its upscale credentials without being stuffy. Think of the moment in a great Mexico City bar when the bartender — who knows every mezcalero by first name — tells you a dicho about the drink they just put in front of you.

The graphic identity runs with editorial polish (restrained grids, refined serifs, controlled palette) to signal seriousness; the activations run with confident energy (oversized type, bold color blocks, after-hours moodiness) to signal life.

**The tension that makes Blue Mezcal work:** editorial discipline ↔ party energy. Never drop either side.

## 2. CREATIVE DIRECTION — THE WORLD IT LIVES IN

**If it was a fashion brand:** Jacquemus × Khaite × a Oaxacan weaver. Tailored cuts, warm palette, Mexican heritage textiles treated with editorial respect.

**If it was a magazine:** a mash-up of *Apartamento*, *Cereal*, and *Mezcalistas* zine. Editorial, design-literate, but with real bar-culture soul.

**If it was a physical space beyond the restaurant:** a candlelit mezcaleria in Roma Norte at 11pm, or a sunlit tasting room in Oaxaca at 3pm. Paper-cream walls, hand-thrown clay, single agave plant in the corner, hand-hammered brass, linen napkins, vintage Milagros glass, a Casa Azul tile behind the bar.

**If it was a film:** Y Tu Mamá También × Roma × Call My Agent! — Mexican cultural warmth + contemporary cosmopolitan humor + brief flashes of documentary-observed intimacy.

**If it was a song:** Natalia Lafourcade's studio session recorded in the bar after-hours.

## 3. THE TENSIONS (what makes it NOT just one thing)

| Tension | How it shows up | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Editorial polish ↔ party energy | Refined serif + oversized loud word | Signals "serious kitchen, fun bar" in one look |
| Mexican heritage ↔ cosmopolitan now | Agave-worm silhouette + GT Sectra | Honors tradition, speaks to today's diner |
| Daylight ↔ nocturnal | Bone-cream by day, charred black by night | Matches the dining arc of the space |
| Bilingual accent ↔ English primary | One Spanish word per post, never three | Avoids tokenism, honors the culture |
| Food reverence ↔ cocktail cheek | Dishes plated editorially, drinks treated with wordplay | Two personalities, one brand |

## 4. VOICE

**One voice, three modes:**

**Food voice** — short-form reverent. Lets the plate speak.
- *"Smoke, citrus, slow."*
- *"Impossible to have one."*
- *"Cool and dangerous."*
- *"Para todo mal, mezcal."*

**Cocktail voice** — bartender-confident, insider-casual. OK to be wry. A well-earned one-liner is better than a description.
- *"Smoke, citrus, slow."*
- *"We don't serve margaritas. We serve arguments."*
- *"Yes, it's strong. That's the point."*

**Utility voice** — direct, warm, never corporate. If you have to announce something logistical (DoorDash, hours, a menu drop), do it with taste.
- *"The new menu drops Thursday. Don't wait for Friday."*
- *"Now on DoorDash. Yes, even the Mezcal Negroni. (No mezcal on DoorDash. Joking.)"*

**Bilingual rule:** one Spanish word or phrase per post. Italic. As accent, never primary. Cultural holidays flip it — Spanish primary, English italic beneath.

**Forbidden voice patterns:** no generic "foodie" lines, no exclamation points, no "tag someone who," no emoji chains. One emoji, earned.

## 5. CONTENT ENGINE — 9 POST TYPES

Every post belongs to one of these types. Each has a purpose, visual direction, trigger, and approximate feed share.

### 5.1 Cocktail Spotlight — *25% of feed*
- **Purpose:** showcase one drink, transfer drink-forward reputation
- **Visual direction:** portrait crop, rim-lit glass, 60% negative space, brass or cobalt accent type, single dicho or mood line as copy
- **Trigger:** new cocktail, menu rotation, featured drink, bartender's choice
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** nocturnal or playful (mood-dependent)

### 5.2 Hero Dish Editorial — *20% of feed*
- **Purpose:** showcase one dish with Pujol-level reverence
- **Visual direction:** 3/4 or overhead plate on cobalt or bone ground, 65% negative space, GT Sectra headline + Söhne small-caps subhead
- **Trigger:** new dish, menu launch, chef's special
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** editorial or playful

### 5.3 Ingredient / Process Anatomy — *10% of feed*
- **Purpose:** mezcal-education, agave-heritage, process respect
- **Visual direction:** hand-drawn callouts + labeled arrows pointing to botanicals, rim treatments, ice types. Not busy — 3–5 labels max. Italic serif labels + hairline curved-arrow (Cruffin move).
- **Trigger:** mezcal-geek content, new technique, ingredient story
- **Aspect:** 4:5 or 1:1
- **Register:** editorial

### 5.4 Typography-First Post — *10% of feed*
- **Purpose:** break the photo rhythm, let language carry weight
- **Visual direction:** NO PHOTO. Full-canvas type. A dicho, a menu quote, a cocktail name as masthead. Oversized GT Sectra italic or cobalt serif caps. Single-color field with 5% tonal gradient.
- **Trigger:** dicho posts, quote posts, "new menu" reveal, event typography
- **Aspect:** 4:5 or 1:1
- **Register:** editorial

### 5.5 Promo / Event Flyer — *10% of feed*
- **Purpose:** announce events, drops, happy hour, residencies
- **Visual direction:** poster-level design (not cheap Canva). Masthead-style headline, illustrated or photo hero, small-caps date/time/price lockup, hairline-ruled info strip
- **Trigger:** Mezcal Mondays, guest bartender, Día de Muertos, cocktail class
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** either

### 5.6 Location / Map Story — *5% of feed*
- **Purpose:** reinforce "where to find us," bring DE neighborhood into the brand
- **Visual direction:** illustrated map — hand-drawn vector Middletown with Blue Mezcal as a marked landmark. Cobalt ink on bone-cream ground. Agave plant + milagro cross + Casa Azul tile scattered as waypoints. Script "You are here" annotation.
- **Trigger:** grand openings, new patio, neighborhood event, "first time visitor" post, cross-promo with Azteca
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** editorial-illustrated

### 5.7 Utility / Operational — *7% of feed*
- **Purpose:** DoorDash-available, hours, reservations, weather-delay, holiday closure
- **Visual direction:** designed as intentional poster, NEVER plain text. Small-caps dateline + hairline rule + cocktail-menu aesthetic. Example: "Now On DoorDash." in GT Sectra, marigold hairline underline, small italic "(Yes, including the guac.)"
- **Trigger:** whenever operational info is needed
- **Aspect:** 4:5 or 1:1
- **Register:** editorial
- **Rule:** utility posts get the SAME design love as hero posts. The brand world doesn't drop for a logistics update.

### 5.8 Personality / Team — *8% of feed*
- **Purpose:** humanize the bar — bartender, chef, owner. Drive "you know them" affinity.
- **Visual direction:** documentary portrait (Portra 800 film grain), 35mm grain, tungsten warm, subject in action (shaking, pouring, plating). Small-caps name + role caption. Single Spanish phrase pull-quote if the person is Mexican.
- **Trigger:** monthly featured team member, "meet the bartender" drop, event coverage
- **Aspect:** 4:5
- **Register:** editorial

### 5.9 Campaign Drop — *5% of feed (used in 3–5 post sequences)*
- **Purpose:** a themed multi-post campaign (not a single post)
- **Visual direction:** see §9 Campaign Structure
- **Trigger:** new-menu launch, seasonal, collab, residency, Día de Muertos
- **Aspect:** consistent within the campaign
- **Register:** campaign-defined

## 6. VISUAL LANGUAGE

### 6.1 Typography — behavior, not just fonts

**Primary hierarchy (all registers):**
- **Hero display** — GT Sectra, Canela, or Tiempos Headline. Italic OK. Tight tracking (-10 to -30 em). Often oversized (40-55% of canvas) when type IS the image.
- **Subhead / label** — Söhne, Neue Haas Grotesk, or GT America small-caps with wide tracking (+40 to +120 em). Used for names, dates, locations.
- **Body** — Söhne or GT America regular, 28-36px equivalent, leading 1.3-1.45.
- **Script accent** — Caslon Italic, Sainte Colombe, Farnham Italic. Used SPARINGLY — one line, pull-quote only. Never as body copy.

**Behavior rules:**
- Type can be oversized enough to clip the photo (Chomp City move) when earned — max once per 6 posts
- Serif italic + sans small-caps together = default editorial lockup
- Script + serif caps together = celebratory lockup (Frenchette move)
- NO Trajan, NO Papyrus, NO Bebas Neue, NO generic "cocktail bar" display fonts

### 6.2 Color usage — strict palette, flexible deployment

**Editorial palette (daylight):** cobalt #1E3A8A · bone cream #F3ECD8 · marigold #F2A900 · agave green #4E6B3A · charred black #1C1A17.
Rule: 60 bone, 30 cobalt, 10 marigold. Black/green as pulls.

**Nocturnal palette (after-hours):** charred black #0B0B0B · brass #C9A24B · velvet burgundy #4A1621 · smoke gray #2A2825 · warm white #EDE4D3.
Rule: 60 black, 30 brass/burgundy, 10 warm white.

**Playful palette (NEW — the "bold sibling of Azteca" mode):** cobalt #1E3A8A · bone cream #F3ECD8 · marigold #F2A900 · chile red #D72638 · agave green #4E6B3A.
Rule: 50 bone, 25 cobalt, 15 marigold, 10 chile-red. For promo, campaign, flyer, cocktail-spotlight when daylight.

**NEVER mix palettes within a single post.** Pick one register per post.

**Photo-led complement rule:** when the dish/drink is a warm hue (chile-red, amber, orange), use cobalt or agave-green as counter. When the drink is a cool hue (lime-green, cucumber, blue), use marigold or chile-red as counter. The design punches OPPOSITE the photo.

### 6.3 Composition DNA

- Asymmetric layouts > symmetric menu-card layouts
- 40-65% canvas fill (editorial restraint, never density for density's sake)
- One oversized element per post — either type OR photo, not both loud
- Type can wrap/frame the photo (Chomp City) OR sit adjacent (Ghia two-field split) — never bisect the hero
- Canvas edge margin minimum 40px on 1080px canvas
- 60-65% negative space as default

### 6.4 Image treatment

**Photography:**
- Portra 400 (daylight scenes) or Portra 800 (nocturnal) film grain emulation
- Bright editorial daylight 5200K for food hero
- Tungsten 2800-3000K for bar / after-hours
- Moody rim-lit (brass or amber) for cocktail spotlights at night
- Never clean digital; always grain

**Real-image identity lock applies always** — the dish photo is the real dish photo, never regenerated. Design composes around it.

### 6.5 Graphic elements — the ornament library

**Hand-drawn marks (optional, ONE per layout max, default zero):**
- Agave leaf or whole-plant silhouette (inked hairline)
- Citrus wedge cross-section
- Salt-rim arc
- Smoke curl
- Ice-cube cube outline
- Cocktail stirrer line
- Mezcal worm silhouette
- Milagros cross (small)
- Papel picado corner cutout (sparingly, editorial version — not busy)

**Textures:**
- 3-8% noise/grain overlay on every flat color field (non-negotiable)
- Handmade paper grain on editorial register
- Brass-foil specular on nocturnal register
- Tonal gradient 5-12% shift on every color field (never flat hex)

**Forbidden:**
- Drop shadows (unless pro-spec — y4-8, blur16-40, opacity 8-20%, darker-shade not black)
- Photoshop default glows
- Stock "fiesta" papel picado clip-art
- Gradient meshes
- Gradient-color-change swooshes

## 8. VARIATION LOGIC — how the feed breathes

The feed should never repeat the same post-type two weeks in a row without at least 2 other types in between. Rough target mix across any 10-post window:

- **40% structured** — Hero Dish, Cocktail Spotlight in standard editorial compositions
- **25% experimental** — Typography-First, Oversized-type-wraps-photo, Recipe Sidebar, Dramatic Crop
- **20% campaign** — part of a 3-5 post campaign sequence
- **10% utility** — DoorDash, hours, reservations — DESIGNED
- **5% disruptive** — something that breaks BM's own rules deliberately (e.g. a one-color-ink Frenchette post, a fully-illustrated Pine & Crane-style breakfast painting, a brutalist all-type wallpaper)

The disruptive 5% is non-negotiable. If the feed doesn't break its own rules occasionally, it stops feeling alive.

## 9. CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE — 3-5 post sequences

A campaign is not a single post — it is 3-5 posts that share a visual thread but evolve.

**Pattern A — New-cocktail launch (4 posts):**
1. **Teaser typography** — a dicho or quote that hints at the drink (typography-first, no photo). *"Some drinks are arguments."*
2. **Reveal** — hero cocktail spotlight. Full shot, name, 1-line description.
3. **Ingredient anatomy** — labeled-diagram post showing the build.
4. **Customer reaction / video** — a reel of it being shaken, handed across the bar.

**Pattern B — Menu-drop campaign (5 posts, over 2 weeks):**
1. Masthead reveal — "New Menu · May 5" in GT Sectra, cobalt on bone, no photo
2. Hero food shot (#1 signature dish)
3. Hero cocktail shot (#1 signature drink)
4. Behind-the-scenes reel — chef + bartender
5. Menu-carousel (editorial grid of 4-6 items as a multi-frame carousel)

**Pattern C — Cultural / Día de Muertos (3 posts):**
1. Altar / ofrenda editorial photograph, Spanish-primary copy
2. A drink on the ofrenda (single cocktail, candlelight)
3. A dicho typography-first post in cobalt with a marigold pull-quote

## 10. COHESION RULE — the ONE thing that makes every post recognizable as Blue Mezcal

**The Blue Mezcal cohesion signature is three things, at least two of which must appear in every post:**

1. **A refined serif in italic OR small-caps** — at some scale, somewhere. Never absent.
2. **A controlled palette pull** — cobalt, bone-cream, marigold, charred-black, brass, or velvet-burgundy (one register per post, never mixed).
3. **A 5-12% tonal gradient on at least one color field** — never a pure flat fill.

Other brand-world elements (agave ornament, Spanish phrase, film grain) are optional per-post; the three above are the non-negotiable recognition signal.

**The "one-second test":** when a Blue Mezcal post lands in the feed between a Frenchette post and a Sweetgreen post, a design-literate person should identify it as Blue Mezcal in under one second based on the palette + type alone.

## 11. CONTENT-TYPE-SPECIFIC RULES

### 11.1 Cocktail Spotlight
- MUST use photo as hero
- Headline choice is dish-name (a) OR feeling line (b) OR voice-seed personality (c) — mix across a variation set
- Glass must be rim-lit if nocturnal register
- Allowed experimental moves: Frenchette cut-out on cream, oversized-type-wraps-glass, dramatic rim close-up
- Forbidden: hand-drawn arrows into the drink (that's AZ)

### 11.2 Hero Dish Editorial
- Photo is hero, 55% canvas minimum
- Type takes supporting role
- Approved layouts: two-field split, vertical strip, dateline + hero
- The dish does the talking — trust it

### 11.3 Ingredient Anatomy
- Labeled-diagram format — 3-5 labels, italic serif, hairline curved arrows
- Never more than 5 labels (Cruffin rule: fewer is more)
- Each label earns its place — if it's just restating what's visible, cut it
- Supports mezcal education and process-respect voice

### 11.4 Typography-First Post
- NO photo
- Full-canvas type, oversized
- Copy is a dicho, a quote, a menu name, or a single loud statement
- Single-color field, 5% tonal gradient mandatory
- Hairline rule + dateline OK as accent

### 11.5 Promo / Event Flyer
- Masthead lockup top (venue name + event name)
- Hero image or illustration middle
- Small-caps info lockup bottom (date, time, price)
- Hairline rule separating sections
- Never cram — 60% negative space even on flyers

### 11.6 Location / Map Story
- Hand-drawn vector map of Middletown DE (or wider DE region)
- Cobalt ink on bone-cream ground (editorial palette default)
- Blue Mezcal marked with a small agave-silhouette pin
- Other neighborhood landmarks marked as minor hairline silhouettes (church, town hall, river)
- Script "You are here" annotation at the BM location
- Map can include a small compass rose, a scale bar, and a tiny "From" line like vintage tourism brochures
- Rendering style: hand-drawn vector, NOT unreal-engine (save that for Azteca)

### 11.7 Utility / Operational
- Same visual language as editorial posts — no degradation
- Small-caps dateline top
- Loud-but-elegant headline middle ("Now On DoorDash.")
- Small italic humor line or dicho below, if earned
- Hairline rule footer with address/CTA
- Forbidden: phone-screenshot UI, generic "we're open" clip-art

### 11.8 Personality / Team
- Portra 800 grain mandatory
- Subject in ACTION, not posed
- Small-caps name + role caption bottom-left or bottom-right
- Optional: one pull-quote in script italic (the person's own words)
- Aspect: 4:5 preferred

### 11.9 Campaign Drop
- Visual thread locked at campaign start (palette, type lockup, ornament)
- Each post within evolves the thread
- Campaign closes with a call-back to post #1 visual language
- Minimum 3, maximum 5 posts per campaign

## 12. SEASONAL / OCCASIONAL OVERRIDES

- **Día de Muertos (Nov 1-2):** Spanish primary, English italic beneath. Marigold-dominant palette. Ofrenda imagery allowed.
- **Cinco de Mayo:** rejected as a promotional frame (not a real Mexican holiday of reverence). Replace with Mexican Independence Day (Sept 16) if seasonal promo is needed.
- **Valentine's Day:** velvet-burgundy palette OK. Paired-drinks visual.
- **Summer patio:** editorial-playful palette, Portra 400 bright daylight.
- **Winter (Dec-Feb):** nocturnal-register dominant, brass and candlelight.
