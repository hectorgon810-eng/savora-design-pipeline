## Two palettes (pick one per post — never mix)

### A. Editorial palette (daylight / service hours)
Use for hero dishes, menu reveals, editorial carousels, daytime scenes.

| Role | Hex | Name |
|---|---|---|
| Secondary dominant | `#1E3A8A` | Cobalt |
| Canvas | `#F3ECD8` | Bone cream |
| Accent | `#F2A900` | Marigold |

Use for bar-focused content, late-night cocktail spotlights, rim-lit glassware.

| Role | Hex | Name |
|---|---|---|

**Rule:** 60 black, 30 brass/burgundy mix, 10 warm white.

## Typography

| Role | Fonts | Prompt descriptor |
|---|---|---|
| Editorial display | Canela · GT Sectra · Domaine Display · Tiempos Headline · Recoleta | "refined editorial serif with tight tracking" |
| Nocturnal display | Bodoni 72 · Didot · GT Sectra Fine · Ogg | "editorial serif italic, high-contrast display" |
| Sans | Söhne · Neue Haas Grotesk · GT America · ABC Diatype | "quiet modern grotesque small-caps" |
| Script | Caslon Italic · Sainte Colombe | "refined italic, accent only — pull-quotes" |

Sizes: display 120–200px · subhead 48–72px · body 28–36px.
Tracking: display tight (−10 to −30 em), small-caps labels wide (+40 to +120 em).
Leading: display 1.05–1.15 · body 1.3–1.45.

## Visual language

- Noise/grain overlay 3–8% opacity on every flat colour field
- Subtle handmade paper grain
- Tonal gradient (5% shift) on solid colour fields — never flat
- Film grain (Portra 800) on photography
- Moody low-light rim-lit cocktail photography with brass or amber rim-light
- Chef portrait / pass-level documentary photography
- 60–65% negative space as the default
